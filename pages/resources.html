The Capstone Paper

This research is the primary academic source behind this website. Written as a Capstone Semester 1 project at Oakland Technical High School, the paper synthesizes 24 academic sources into a comprehensive framework for building a monetizable brand in the digital economy.

Capstone Research Paper · MLA Format

How to Build a Monetizable Brand and a Sustainable Business in Today's Digital Economy

A 9+ page academic paper covering brand orientation, digital marketing, monetization frameworks, e-commerce operations, community building, legal protection, financial planning, and case studies of Glossier, Pollo Campero, Heineken, and Coca-Cola.

Recommended Books

Further Reading

Building a StoryBrand — Donald Miller Practical framework for clarifying your brand message and communicating value. Essential for brand identity and value proposition work.
Subscribed — Tien Tzuo Deep-dive into subscription business model strategy. Covers pricing, retention, and scaling recurring revenue.
Business Model Generation — Alexander Osterwalder The definitive guide to the Business Model Canvas framework referenced throughout the capstone paper. Practical for early-stage entrepreneurs mapping value creation systems.
Contagious — Jonah Berger Research-backed framework for understanding why content and brands go viral. Directly relevant to the digital marketing and community-building sections of the paper.
Academic Sources

Key Research Articles

Selected highlights from the 24 sources cited in the paper. View the full Works Cited →

Libai et al. — Influencer Marketing Unlocked (2025) Introduces an equity-driven framework analyzing how influencers contribute to customer equity and platform value. Journal of the Academy of Marketing Science.
doi.org/10.1007/s11747-024-01073-2
Rieder et al. — Making a Living in the Creator Economy (2023) Large-scale study of how YouTube creators link to external platforms to diversify income. Social Media + Society.
doi.org/10.1177/20563051231180628
Weland et al. — Subscription-Based Business Models (2024) Examines how tech firms capture value through agile subscription infrastructure. International Journal of Industrial Engineering and Operations Management.
doi.org/10.1108/IJIEOM-06-2023-0054
Ascarza et al. — In Pursuit of Enhanced Customer Retention (2018) Comprehensive framework for identifying at-risk customers and designing retention campaigns. Customer Needs and Solutions, Springer.
doi.org/10.1007/s40547-017-0080-0
Hödl & Myrach — Content Creators Between Platform Control and User Autonomy (2023) Examines how algorithmic control creates income volatility for digital creators. Business & Information Systems Engineering.
doi.org/10.1007/s12599-023-00808-9
